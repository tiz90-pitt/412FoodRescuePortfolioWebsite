About This Campaign

Pittsburgh has a thriving cultural community most locals walk past without understanding.

The Culture Spotlight campaign is a multi-platform narrative initiative for the Pittsburgh Cultural Center (PCCC), designed to bridge the cultural context gap between PCCC's performances and the broader Pittsburgh community.

While PCCC produces vibrant, high-quality cultural events, their current communications function primarily as event recaps for existing community members. This campaign reframes PCCC's content around a curiosity-first, editorial tone that speaks outward to non-heritage audiences — without alienating the existing community.

Every component functions as a chapter in one story: a curious Pittsburgher encounters something visually striking, learns what it means, feels welcomed into the community, and eventually advocates for it.

"Like a friend explaining their heritage — warm, specific, story-forward."

About PCCC

The community behind the community.

The Pittsburgh Cultural Center (PCCC) is a nonprofit that promotes cultural awareness and diversity through involvement with the greater Pittsburgh community. Founded in 2015 following the success of the community-organized Cultural Festival, PCCC has grown into a platform for anyone to share talents and exchange culture.

PCCC hosts two major annual events: the Pittsburgh Cultural Festival and the Greater Pittsburgh New Year Gala. Their art troupe includes a chorus, folk-dance team, Qipao team, and drama club, each engaging with Pittsburgh on a variety of stages throughout the year.
