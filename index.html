About This Campaign

Addressing the "logistics gap" with a heartbeat.

This campaign shifts the perception of food rescue away from a purely logistical equation of "pounds and miles" and redefines it as a "living network" of human connection. By framing every individual food rescue as a "heartbeat," the campaign illustrates how these actions sustain the overall health of the Pittsburgh community.

The core conflict addresses a severe "logistics gap": 40% of food is wasted, yet one in seven residents faces daily hunger. This crisis is exacerbated by the 2026 economic climate, where surging grocery prices have drastically increased the demand for food access among vulnerable and homebound populations.

Our goal is to evolve the volunteer base from "transactional" app-based activism into a unified Advocate Network that fights for systemic, long-term policy changes and combats "outreach fatigue."

Organization Background

Bridging the gap since 2015.

412 Food Rescue is a non-profit organization dedicated to preventing food waste and hunger while promoting environmental sustainability. It was established in response to the "logistics gap" in Pittsburgh's food ecosystem.

The organization's mission is to quickly rescue surplus fresh food and deliver it to those in need. They achieve this by leveraging technology and fostering strong community partnerships to combat food insecurity and climate change.

Since launching their innovative Food Rescue Hero app in 2016, operations have transformed, scaling their impact across North America.
